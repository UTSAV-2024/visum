# Product

## Register

brand

## Platform

web

## Users
Store owners, indie founders, and agency operators who suspect AI assistants (ChatGPT, Perplexity, Claude) can't see their site. Not deeply technical; they arrive anxious and curious, usually from a social post or word of mouth, on desktop or phone. The job to be done: "tell me if AI can read my site, and what to fix, without making me feel stupid."

## Product Purpose
Visum scans any public website in ~20 seconds against 8 technical checks (robots.txt, JSON-LD, llms.txt, MCP endpoint, JS rendering, meta tags, sitemap, speed) and returns a 0–100 AI-visibility score with a prioritized fix list. Success: a visitor runs a scan within 30 seconds of landing, understands their score, and either fixes an issue or saves the report.

## Positioning
The diagnostic X-ray for the AI web: SEO tools measure rankings for humans; Visum measures whether machines can read you at all.

## Conversion & proof
- Primary CTA: run a free scan (URL input, no signup).
- Secondary CTA: view a sample report.
- The line a visitor remembers: "My website might be invisible to AI — and I can see exactly why in 20 seconds."
- Belief ladder: (1) AI assistants are becoming how people find things → (2) those assistants read sites differently than humans do → (3) my site probably fails some of those machine checks → (4) this scan shows me precisely what's broken, free, right now.
- Proof on hand: the live scan itself is the proof (results in seconds, real findings per site). No testimonials yet — do not fabricate any.

## Brand Personality
Diagnostic, candid, precise. The voice of a good radiologist: calm authority, no fearmongering, no hype adjectives. Emotions to evoke: clarity and agency ("now I know exactly what to do"), a little awe at seeing the machine's-eye view of one's own site.

## Anti-references
- Generic AI-SaaS template: purple-gradient-on-black, glassmorphism cards, gradient text, sparkle emojis.
- SEO-tool clutter: dashboard chrome, dense stat tiles, upgrade nags on every scroll.
- Fear-based marketing: red warnings and doom copy to scare users into converting.

## Design Principles
- Practice what you preach: the site itself must pass its own scanner; machine-readable and fast is part of the brand story.
- The scan is the hero: every screen exists to get a URL scanned or a result understood; nothing competes with that.
- Show the machine's-eye view: visualizations should feel like seeing your site the way a crawler sees it — that's the memorable moment.
- Candor over drama: findings are stated plainly with severity earned by evidence, never inflated to scare.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text ≥4.5:1 contrast. Full reduced-motion alternatives for every animation. Keyboard-completable scan flow. Score never communicated by color alone (always paired with number + label).
